# Instant Pot Jackfruit Clam Chowder (Hybrid Method)

*Instant Pot, jackfruit, chowder, soup, vegan*

**4-6 Servings**

---

- *1 (20 oz)* can young green jackfruit in brine (not syrup)
- *3.5 C* water
- *1.5-2 T* Better Than Bouillon Vegetarian No-Chicken Base
- *1 (3-4 inch)* piece dried kombu seaweed
- *1 T* olive oil
- *1* large yellow onion, diced
- *2* celery stalks, diced
- *2* cloves garlic, minced
- *2* large Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes (about 1.5 lbs), peeled and diced into 1/2-inch cubes
- *1-1.5 t* Spike seasoning, plus more to taste
- *1/2 t* black pepper, plus more to taste
- *1/4 t* salt, plus more to taste
- *2 C* unsweetened plain plant-based milk (oat, cashew, or soy)
- *2 T* cornstarch
- *1 T* fresh lemon juice
- fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish (optional)

---

### Prep the Jackfruit

Drain and thoroughly rinse the canned jackfruit under cold running
water to remove the briny tang. Using your fingers or two forks, pull
apart the jackfruit pieces to shred them into “clam-like” strands. Set
aside.

### Sauté Aromatics

Press the **Sauté** button on your Instant Pot and set it to
**Normal**. Add the olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the diced
onion and celery. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened (about
5-7 minutes). Add the minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute until
fragrant. Press **Cancel** to turn off Sauté mode.

### Pressure Cook the Kombu Broth

Pour in about 1/2 cup of the water and use a wooden spoon to
thoroughly scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the inner
pot. This deglazing step prevents a “Burn” error later. Add the
remaining 3 cups of water and the Better Than Bouillon base, stirring
until dissolved. Drop the piece of kombu into the liquid. Secure the
lid and ensure the venting knob is set to **Sealing**. Select **Manual
/ Pressure Cook** on **High Pressure** for **3 minutes**. When the
timer goes off, perform a **Quick Release (QR)** by carefully
switching the valve to Venting. Once the pin drops, safely open the
lid.

### Build the Chowder & Slow Cook

Carefully fish out the piece of kombu with tongs and discard it. Add
the shredded jackfruit, diced potatoes, Spike seasoning, black pepper,
and initial salt to the pot. Stir to combine. (Do not add the plant
milk or cornstarch yet). Place a glass lid on the Instant Pot (or use
the standard lid set to the **Venting** position). Select the **Slow
Cook** function and set it to **More** (High) for **1.5 to 2 hours**,
or until the potatoes are easily pierced with a fork.

### Thicken on Low Heat

In a separate bowl, whisk together the 2 cups of plant-based milk and
2 tablespoons of cornstarch until completely smooth with no remaining
lumps. Pour this slurry into the hot chowder, stirring vigorously as
you pour to integrate it completely. Press **Cancel** to stop the Slow
Cook function, then press **Sauté** and immediately adjust the
temperature setting to **Less** (Low heat). Cook for 5 to 7 minutes,
**stirring constantly** with a silicone spatula or wooden spoon,
making sure to scrape the bottom. The chowder will gently thicken into
a rich, creamy texture without scorching.

### Finish and Serve

Press **Cancel** to turn off the heat. Stir in the 1 tablespoon of
fresh lemon juice. Taste the broth and adjust your seasonings, adding
a bit more Spike, pepper, or salt if needed. Ladle into bowls, garnish
with fresh parsley, and serve hot alongside crackers or crusty bread.
