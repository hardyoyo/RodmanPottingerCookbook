# Soybean Paste Stew

from _Korean Vegetarian Cooking_ by Shin Kim

*Korean, soup, vegan*

**8 Servings**

---

- *4 T* Korean fermented soybean paste (doenjang)
- *2 T* toasted sesame seed oil
- *4 t* garlic, minced
- *2* medium potatoes, cut into small dice (don't peel)
- *2* medium zucchini, cut into small dice
- *8* oz. of button mushrooms, cut into medium dice
- *7-8 C* vegetable stock, or water and bouillon
- *1* block of extra firm tofu, pressed and cut into medium dice
- *1* jalapeño pepper, thinly sliced, optional, for garnish
- Jasmine rice, for serving

---

Start the sauté on *low heat*. Add the sesame oil first, then slowly
stir in the soybean paste and garlic. Keep the heat low — the paste
will burn if you rush it. Once the paste is incorporated, add the
potato, zucchini and mushrooms, stir to coat. Add stock/water and
bring to boil over medium heat. Cook until the potato is soft, about
10 minutes. Check often, skim off any foam if it’s worrisome, but
usually there’s not any foam, and it’s no big deal if there’s a little
bit of foam. Add tofu, bring to boil again, remove from heat, serve
hot with rice, garnish with peppers for anyone who wants to add some
heat or a tasty/peppery crunch.

> ![info](./images/info-icon.png) *This soup has a reputation for
being “stinky”, mostly because the soybean paste can have a very
powerful smell, especially when you’re starting out with the soup. It
will quickly moderate, and smell really amazing. Trust us. Some
versions of soybean paste have been moderated for western kitchens,
but, really, if it smells like a lot, that’s a good sign, you’re
cooking with authentic Korean soybean paste. Gaja!*
