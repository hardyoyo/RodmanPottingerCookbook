# Gochujang Noodles

*noodles, korean, spicy, vegan, dinner*

**3 Servings**

---

- *16 oz* fresh udon noodles (or 24 oz frozen, or 8-9 oz dried)
- *3 T* gochujang
- *2 T* soy sauce (use tamari for GF)
- *2 T* mirin (or rice vinegar; if using vinegar, use 2 T sugar)
- *1 1/2 T* brown sugar
- *1 T* gochugaru (optional)
- *1 T* toasted sesame oil
- *1 heaping T* neutral oil
- *1 bunch* scallions, whites/light greens chopped 1", dark greens reserved
- *4* garlic cloves, chopped
- *1 inch* ginger, grated
- *6-8 C* Napa cabbage, thinly sliced (or 2 bell peppers, sliced)
- *1/4 C* roasted white sesame seeds
- *1 C* Thai basil or cilantro, loosely packed
- *1 t* toasted sesame oil

---

Cook noodles per package instructions but 1 minute less. Drain, reserving some noodle water.

Whisk gochujang, soy sauce, mirin, brown sugar, gochugaru, and toasted sesame oil in a bowl.

Heat neutral oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add scallion whites/light greens, garlic, and ginger. Cook 1-2 minutes. Add Napa cabbage with a pinch of salt and stir-fry 3 minutes until tender but not soft.

Add gochujang sauce and sesame seeds; let sizzle. Add noodles and toss to coat. Add a splash of noodle water if dry (more likely with dried noodles). Cook 1-2 minutes until sauce clings.

Off heat, stir in Thai basil and a drizzle of toasted sesame oil.
