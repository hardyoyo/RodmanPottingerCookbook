# Maafe (West African Peanut Stew)

From Lahb Co. Eats, by Nasim Lahbichi.

*stew, African, vegan, peanut*

**4-6 Servings**

---

- *1* red onion, diced
- *1 1/2 T* ginger, minced
- *3* garlic cloves, minced
- *2 T* neutral oil
- *1-2* sweet potatoes, cubed
- *1* 14.5 oz can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- *2* 14 oz cans fire roasted tomatoes
- *2/3 C* natural creamy peanut butter
- *1 can* water (use the empty tomato can)
- *1 T* Better Than Bouillon (roasted vegetable or vegetable base)
- *1/2 lb* hearty greens (collards, kale, or swiss chard), chopped
- *1/4 C* cilantro, chopped
- *1* lime, juiced
- *1/2 C* roasted peanuts, chopped, for garnish
- *1 t* coriander
- *1 t* cumin
- *1/2 t* cayenne
- *1/4 t* cinnamon
- *1 t* salt
- *1/2 t* black pepper

---

Stir the spice blend together in a small bowl.

Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic,
and ginger and sauté until softened, about 3 minutes. Add the spice
blend and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.

Pour in the can of water and scrape up any bits. Add the Better Than
Bouillon, fire roasted tomatoes, chickpeas, and sweet potatoes. Season
with salt and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20 minutes,
stirring occasionally. If the stew thickens too much, add more water a
splash at a time.

Scoop about 1/2 cup of the hot liquid into a bowl and whisk with the
peanut butter until smooth. Stir the mixture back into the pot. Cover
and cook 5 minutes more.

Remove from heat. Stir in the greens, cilantro, and lime juice until
the greens wilt. Serve over rice, topped with chopped peanuts.
