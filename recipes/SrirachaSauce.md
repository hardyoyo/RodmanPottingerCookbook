# Homemade Sriracha Sauce

From: https://www.allrecipes.com/recipe/235276/how-to-make-homemade-sriracha-sauce/

*Condiment, Spicy, vegan*

**24 Servings (1 1/2 cups)**

---

- *1 lb* red jalapeño peppers, stems removed
- *1/2 lb* red serrano peppers, stems removed
- *1/3 C* water
- *4 cloves* garlic, peeled
- *3 T* light brown sugar
- *1 T* kosher salt
- *1/2 C* distilled white vinegar

---

Place jalapeño and serrano peppers into a blender with water, garlic, brown
sugar, and salt. Pulse several times, then blend until smooth.

Transfer puree into a large glass jar or pitcher. Cover with plastic wrap and
place in a cool, dark location for 3 to 5 days, stirring and scraping down the
sides once a day. The mixture will begin to bubble and ferment. Rewrap after
every stirring.

Pour fermented mixture back into the blender; add vinegar and blend until
smooth. Strain through a fine-mesh strainer into a saucepan, pushing as much
pulp through as possible. Discard remaining pulp, seeds, and skin.

Bring sauce to a boil over medium heat, stirring often, until reduced to your
desired thickness, about 5 to 10 minutes. Let cool to room temperature (it will
thicken a little as it cools). Transfer to jars or bottles and refrigerate.

Source: https://www.allrecipes.com/recipe/235276/how-to-make-homemade-sriracha-sauce/

### NOTES

Other additions:

- If you’re short on hot peppers, you can “goose” the heat with Korean Red Pepper
  Flakes or Sambal Olek.
- Add *1 t* Smoked Paprika for a smoky flavor.
- Add *1/2 t* cocoa powder for an earthy note.
- Don’t toss the pulp after straining. Save it in a small glass jelly jar, stir
  in about *1 t* agave syrup, and call it “pepper jelly” — it’s great on eggs!
