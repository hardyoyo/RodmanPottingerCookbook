# Microwave Poached Eggs

*breakfast, egg*

**1 Serving**

---

- *1-2* large eggs
- *1/2 C* cold water
- salt and pepper, to taste

---

Fill a microwave-safe mug or small ceramic bowl with 1/2 cup cold
water. Crack an egg and gently slip it into the water — the egg should
be fully submerged. Prick the yolk once with a toothpick or fork to
prevent explosions. Cover the mug with a microwave-safe saucer or
plate. Everything that touched the egg (mug, saucer, fork, spoon) is
dishwasher-safe — **perfect for a work-from-home breakfast**.

Microwave on high for 30 seconds, then check. If the white isn't set
yet, continue in 10-second increments until the white is opaque but
the yolk is still soft. Timing depends on your microwave wattage
(typically 60-90 seconds total; with practice, you can remember the
previous total time and start with that).

Use a slotted spoon to lift the egg out, drain on a paper towel, and
season with salt and pepper. Serve immediately on toast, an English
muffin, or whatever you've got handy. You can go all Eggs Benedict
with this if you want, but I prefer to garnish these with some finely
diced jalapeño, and drizzle a bit of sriracha on them.
